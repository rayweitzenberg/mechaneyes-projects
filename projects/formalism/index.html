Formalism

Daylies, 1994-Present

I picked up a camera in 1994, and was immediately enamored. As my very first print was taking shape in the developer tray, a guy next to me took a look and and reacted to it in the same way I had myself. He got the joke! Being painfully shy back then this opened up the door to an alternate means of communication. I could tell jokes without even moving my lips.

I studied photography at Parsons School of Design. And I still shoot every day.

The work below focuses on formal elements of art: line, shape, space, color and texture. That in mind it's not a surprise that I'm attracted to Modernism. I do work in a number of loosely coupled mediums, but this collection of images ties directly to the foundation upon which most of what I do is built.
